Module 03: AI Embeddings & Semantic Mapping

How AI places words on a virtual meaning map, translating language into numerical coordinates to understand relationships between concepts.

▼

📺 1. Learn: Lecture & Course Materials

Class Resources

Download the official presentation explaining word addresses, vector strings, and cluster networks:

Download Day 3 PPTX
Key Concepts Covered
  • What Is an Embedding? A list of numbers that acts as a word's fixed address in a multi-dimensional map. Similar words cluster close together.
  • The Word Galaxy: Real embeddings use 300–1500 numbers per word, visualized in 3D spaces with specialized tools.
  • Keyword vs. Meaning Search: Keyword systems find exact matches; meaning search connects related terms (e.g., 'biryani' surfaces pulao, dum rice).
  • Everyday Integration: Powers YouTube recommendations, Spotify Discover Weekly, Google search, and ChatGPT typo resolution.

Day 09

Module 09: AI Memory Limits & The Creativity Dial

Exploring Context Windows (short-term memory limitations) and Temperature (the mathematical randomness setting that controls creativity).

▼

📺 1. Learn: Lecture & Course Materials

Class Resources

Download Day 9 PPTX
Key Concepts Covered
  • Context Window: A model's short-term memory is bounded by a fixed block size. In long sessions, early details drift out and are forgotten.
  • Temperature 0.0: Focused, literal, highly predictable. Ideal for code and math.
  • Temperature 0.7: Natural, fluid, and comfortable to read — realistic narrative phrasing.
  • Temperature 1.5: Creative, wild, and unpredictable. High risk of breaking formatting rules.

Module 11: RAG Systems & Open-Book AI

Building Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) pipelines that read specific textbooks to answer questions with pinpoint precision.

▼

📺 1. Learn: Lecture & Course Materials

Class Resources

Download Day 11 PPTX
Key Concepts Covered
  • The General AI Deficit: LLMs frequently miss specific definitions, exact formulas, or local textbook chapters required for exam marks.
  • RAG (Look First, Answer Second): Like an open-book exam — the AI searches inside the uploaded document first, extracts relevant snippets, then crafts an answer strictly from that source.
  • The Grounding Habit: If a generated answer matches the literal text of the syllabus, it can be trusted. If it contradicts the textbook, the book wins every time.
  • Ubiquitous Architecture: RAG powers banking assistants, legal databases, and customer support networks globally.

📺 1. Learn: Lecture & Course Materials

Class Resources

Download Day 12 PPTX
Key Concepts Covered

  • The 5-Step Agent Loop: Plan → Act → Check → Improve → Repeat.

Day 13

Module 13: AI Ethics, Bias & Hallucinations

Learning to navigate critical limitations of generative AI — tracking down confident hallucinations, spotting visual deepfakes, and committing to responsible technology use.

▼

📺 1. Learn: Lecture & Course Materials

Class Resources

Download Day 13 PPTX
Key Concepts Covered
  • AI Hallucinations: LLMs are mathematical word predictors, not fact databases. They can state fictional events (like a non-existent 1842 Cricket World Cup) with total confidence.
  • Data Bias: If training data contains one-sided viewpoints, the system naturally repeats those biases.
  • Deepfakes & Synthetic Media: Spotting fakes requires scanning for unnatural facial details, distorted backgrounds, or mismatched vocal timing.
  • The Carefulness Habit: Never trust an AI output blindly. Practice continuous, multi-source verification for critical data.
